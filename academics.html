HEC Paris

Master's in Management, Merit Scholarship

HEC Paris

As of early 2021, I'm a student at HEC Paris, currently in my first year of a Master's in Management. I see the program as a way to deepen my knowledge in fields I am interested in (Tech, Sustainability, Entrepreneurship) and to eventually focus on one of those topics during my M2 year.

As such, the aspects I enjoy most about HEC Paris include its focus on collaboration and team-work as well as its entrepreneurial spirit and recent pivot to sustainability-related courses, allowing me to take electives such as Sustainability Strategy, Hydrogen Markets, and EU Energy Policy. I further had the chance to improve my French via free langauge courses and am currently attending the French-speaking elective "Créer et développer une start-up technologique"

Bocconi Univerity

BSc in Economic and Social Sciences

Bocconi Univerity

From September 2017 until July 2020, I studied at Bocconi Univerity in Milan, completing a Bachelor's in Economic and Social Sciences. It was exciting to be in the pioneering class of this program, which, although its Italian predecessor traces back many decades, was first taught in English and its new revised form. As such, our class benefitted from a cutting-edge curriculum in Economics, Behavioral Studies, Sociology, Philosophy, Law and more, while being based on rigurous quantitative methods.

What I most enjoyed in this holistic teaching was that it allows me to assess the impact of innovative businesses on broader society, which became valuable when I wrote my final paper about financial technology and its effects on industries and customers alike.

San Diego State University

Exchange Semester, Courses in Business and Art

San Diego State University

During the second half of 2019, I took the chance to leave Milan for the USA's sunny westcoast. Studying at SDSU was thus as much a personal as an academic endevour. On the academic side, I enjoyed the American style assessment methods, including oral participation and group work. as well as the more busines-related courses I took there.

On the personal side, I made use of the academic flexibility to take courses in drawing and design as well as surfing and sailing and visited friends all over the West Coast which I had gotten to know over the previous years.


Summer School & Online



London School of Economics

Environmental Economics and Urban Geography

London School of Economics

Coming to London for two summer courses at the London School of Economics was exciting to me for two reasons. First, it gave me the chance to study some topics I that perfectly complemented my Bachelor's but were not offered at Bocconi until the third year. I took in Urban Geography, learning about social struggles in modern cities, and Environmental Economics, which would prove very benefitial in my later studies and extracurricular acitivites at Bocconi and HEC.

Going to London also let me experience another educational system, one that I had already considered for my Bachelor's was considering once again for my Master's. Had it not been for my pivot to business studies and the UK's departure from the EU, it is likely I would have pursued my Master's at LSE as well.

Univerity College London

Future Cities and Energy Systems

Univerity College London

Besides the broader field of social sciences, both architecture and engineering were always a deep fascination of mine. Just like successful businesses in technology and consumer goods, architects and engineers can create lasting things that affect our everyday life.

While I chose not to pursue either as a career, I remained interested in the fields and doing the course "Future Cities and Energy Systems" at UCL was a way for me to pursue this interest alongside my main studies. The course taught us about the challanges in decarbonizing cities, looking at both their building stock and energy infrastructure. In our final project, we reenvisioned the famous Barbican complex in The City of London, built with modern sustainability standards in mind.

Datacamp, Udemy, EdX
