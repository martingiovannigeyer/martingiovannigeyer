Language and Culture

When I left my hometown for Australia in 2016 to learn English, I wouldn't have guessed that five years later, I would have worked and studied in seven countries across four continentsand reach conversational fluency in four languages.

My experience in Australia made me confident to be able to stand on my own feet and my subsequent time in China was wonderful in introducing me to an entirely different culture and mindset from what I was used. This together with my various travels, many of which included less known destinations such as Montengro or Kazakhstan led me to build a truly global mindset as well as a unique cultural competence and extraordinarily diverse group of friends.

Contemporary art and mid-century literature

Art and Society

Art has always helped humanity to critically reflect upon changes in their environment and broader society. As such, I think that in a time of rapid technological change art has a more important role than ever. The image you see at the front depicts a work by Tatsuo Mijayima, which I had the chance to see in the Syndey MCA in 2016. It uses LED lights, an omnipresent symbol of the 21 century, to reflect upon the very essence of human existence.

In a similar fashion, I developed a fascination for mid-century literature, whether Albert Camus, George Orwell, Ayn Rand, or Max Frisch, each of which developed a unique style of describing and critizicing the changes in society occuring at the time.
