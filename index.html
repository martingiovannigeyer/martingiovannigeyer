Hi, Ciao, Hallo!

This website was born out of a desire to express myself. Having a variety of different interests, I often think that having to condense my life to a one-page CV makes it difficult to let me adequately communicate the links between the different steps of my life.

Take the following as an attempt to bring some of this nuance back, letting you zero in on the aspect that matter most to you, and to introduce myself, as both the Bocconi alumnus and HEC student , but also the the freelance journalist, the marathon-runner, and the web developer that coded this page.


A brief summary



Beginnings

Upbringing and high school

Beginnings

Born and raised in southern Germany, I grew up far from the big cities and diverse environments that would later dominate my life. My biggest hobbies growing up were tennis, track and field, piano, and photography.

In high school, I focused on political studies and physics, while taking a number of electives, as well as participating in the student counctil and the school newspaper. I was a good student and finished fourth among ~100 students at high school graduation, with special honors in physics and history.

Explorations

Gap year in Syndey, Berlin & Shanghai
Studying Economics and more @ Bocconi, LSE, UCL

Explorations

After highschool, I took a gap year allowing me to work and live in Syndey, Berlin, and Shanghai and making me curious on what else the world has to offer.

Upon returning to Europe, I wanted to find a way to make sense of people, societies, nations and economies in our globalized world, so in 2017 I started a Bachlor's in Economic and Social Science at Bocconi University. Subjects ranged from quantitative methods and computer science to philosophy, law, cognitive sciences, and sociology, complemented by summer courses in environmental studies, geography and urban planning that I took at UCL and LSE in London.

Focus

Studying business during exchnage @ SDSU
Learning French, starting MiM @ HEC Paris

Focus

Towards the end of my Bachelor years, I came to incresingly seek practical applications for the skills and knowledge I acquired over the years. During my exchange semester in San Diego, I took classes in Marketing and Finance and discovered business studies as a way to do exactly that, putting knwoledge into practise in various projects while letting me making use of my strengths in public speaking and team building.

Upon returning to Milan, I finished my Bachelor's by writing a final paper about financial technology and its effects on business and society before moving to France to pursue a Master's in Management at HEC Paris.


Some highlights along the way



Gap Year

Learning English while living in Sydey, Shanghai, and Berlin

Gap Year

After repeatedly being encouraged by friends and family to consider studying abroad, I took a gap year, combining my endevour to learn English (ironically my worst subject in high school) with my childhood dream of going to Sydney. Besides my language studies I started working as a retail associate for Sunglass Hut.

I also used the year off to complete my first two internships, and travel through Europe, Australia, and China, mostly by train. In one trip, I was following the steps of ancient traders along the silk road, visiting places from Xi'an over Jiayuguan until Turpan, and up to the border with Kazakhstan.

First Work Experience

Internships @ Voith Group and Junge Liberale e.V.

First Work Experience

I collected some first work experience by interning at the Shanghai office of Voith Group, a German MNE, which was a big step, teaching me the functioning of large MNEs while exposing me to an different culture and work mentality.

Afterwards, I moved to Berlin to work for the German Liberal Party (FDP) on their election campaign in 2017. I started to become involved in politics the year prior during local elections and saw the internship as an opportunity to leverage the small party size to get a deep insight into the German capital's inner workings.

Extracurricular Activities

Head of Communications @ Green Light for Business
Studying Web Design, Data Analysis and Machine Learning

Extracurricular Activities

At Bocconi, I started freelancing for Handelsblatt, Germany's leading financial newspaper, and became involved in the student associations nextPa, where I served as Head of Smart Cities, and Green Light for Business, where I was Communications Manager, leading a team of up to 20 students for almost two years.

I continued to practice sports every other day and started practicing CrossFit and Yoga while in Milan, and going Sailing and Surfing during my exchange in San Diego.

Moving to Paris

Master's in Management @ HEC Paris

Moving to Paris

It was the third time for me moving to a different country without (fluently) speaking the langauge, but many ways the most symbolic. For once, it shows the value of opportunities in ones life: French was a quasi-mandatory subject in my high school but I never chose to study it. When I eventually started my first course in Milan, I couldn't imagine that only shortly after I would become a scholarship student at HEC Paris.

Secondly, it shows that the most fulfilling accomplishments are achieved through courage and dedication. I remember being told that foreign languages are my weak point, now I am conversational in three of them. Having accomplished this makes me just as proud as my academic or extracurricular achievements.
